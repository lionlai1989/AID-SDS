1. What is your preferred language when building predictive models and why?

My preferred language for building and prototyping predictive models is Python. Its vast ecosystem of libraries is helpful for rapid experimentation and fast development iterations.

2. Provide an example of when you used SQL to extract data.

In my role as a Computer Vision Software Engineer at LiveEO, I used PostgreSQL to manage large-scale satellite photogrammetry data. I specifically used geospatial extensions (like PostGIS) to extract imagery based on criteria such as geographic coordinates, geometry, and specific timestamps.

3. Give an example of a situation where you disagreed upon an idea or solution design with a co-worker.  How did you handle the case?

I try to express my ideas clearly so they make sense to my co-worker. If I can find a paper, theoretical proof, or data to present, I will use that as evidence. If not, I rely on my experience from building systems from the ground up to persuade them.

4. What are your greatest strengths and weaknesses and how will these affect your performance here?

My greatest strength is working effectively in multidisciplinary teams. Because of my background in embedded systems, I can collaborate closely with hardware teams to align on technical constraints. Having worked in organizations with flat hierarchies, I am used to communicating with various stakeholders on a daily basis. This has made me capable of breaking down complex engineering ideas into clear concepts for coworkers with non-technical backgrounds, ensuring everyone is aligned on the project goals.

In the past, I have sometimes been too eager to share my technical perspective, which could come across as lecturing rather than collaborating. I realized that this approach can inadvertently stifle my coworkers' creative processes and, more importantly, prevent me from learning from their unique insights. To address this, I am actively practicing active listening and asking more open-ended questions during design reviews. My goal is to create a space where we can share multiple solutions together which ultimately leads to a better result.
